The purpose of this app is to fulfill all cover-collecting needs. However, it can only do that when needs are communicated as application specifications. The app ain't broke if it was never specified to need to do a thing.

A broken app will have symptoms like...

  • Errors in the console.
  • Buttons don't respond.
  • A form gets stuck infinitely loading.
  • A page won't load.
  • Data isn't saved or is lost.

A feature request sounds more like...

  • The app did X, but I wanted Y.
  • There's no way to do X.
  • I don't want X to happen.

Why does the difference matter? I'm the anti-microsoft: I prioritize bug-fixes over features. We want change requests properly identified as bugs or features to ensure bugs get fixed first.
